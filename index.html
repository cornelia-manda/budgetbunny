BUDGETBUNNY

UI/UX Designer / Researcher / Interaction Designer

A financial companion for students, designed to demystify OSAP, track hidden costs, and promote financial literacy without the guilt of a traditional calculator.

My Role

I served as a UI/UX Designer, Researcher, and Interaction Designer for this project, working collaboratively with my team of 3 to bridge the gap between complex financial data and the student experience.

  • User Research: Conducted empathy interviews and synthesized findings into detailed personas to ground our design in real student needs.
  • Prototyping: Led the development of high fidelity interactive flows in Figma.
  • Project Coordination: Orchestrated formal in person work sessions to ensure that every design element was aligned with our research findings.

8 Page Paper: The Problem

Our team of 3 began with an extensive eight page research phase to understand the systematic neglect of financial literacy in education. We conducted interviews with students across various years of study, including international and first generation students.

Key findings revealed that students felt lost and stressed when trying to align OSAP payments with tuition deadlines. Every participant struggled to understand where their money was going, often only reviewing bank statements after a full year of overspending.

Target Personas

Through our collaborative effort, our team developed three primary personas to guide our design decisions. These personas ensured we addressed the specific pain points identified in our interviews.

❮ ❯

Design Process

Our team of 3 followed a rigorous double diamond framework. We successfully fostered a healthy and efficient team environment and attitudes where everyone was comfortable sharing thoughts and opinions. Through productive in person work sessions, we effectively delegated tasks and consistently sought one another's opinions to ensure project cohesion.

This collaborative culture allowed us to seamlessly transition from initial paper sketching and low fidelity task mapping to a robust high fidelity Figma prototype showcasing end to end interactions.

❮ ❯

Visual Identity

Our team utilized a playful identity to make financial management feel less intimidating. The interface uses a consistent mint green palette to reflect growth and financial health.

  • Typography: Kantumruy Pro (Sans-Serif)
  • Palette: Mint Green (#60E4B2) and Radiant Yellow (#F7D368)

Usability & Testing

Following the creation of our high-fidelity prototype, our team of 3 conducted five pilot usability tests. Using the 10 question System Usability Likert Scale, we evaluated the efficiency of the interaction and identified critical friction points before our final presentation.

Key improvements prompted by this testing included clarifying navigational labels to reduce cognitive load and increasing button contrast for accessibility. These iterations ensured the final prototype represented students real world financial needs while reducing the emotional stress of managing scattered deadlines.

Outcome

The project was met with a very positive reaction from the course instructor, ultimately receiving a final grade of over 90 percent. The final interactive prototype successfully addresses the critical financial literacy gaps found in university students by shifting the focus from standard calculation to emotional support and systematic tracking.

While the timeline of the term did not permit final extensive testing, the refined prototype was praised for its intuitive flow and the depth of the research foundation, leaving students feeling significantly more confident in managing hidden costs and tuition deadlines.
